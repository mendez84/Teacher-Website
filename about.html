Meet the Teacher

Mario Mendez

Math & CS Teacher · Palm Springs Unified School District

I teach math because it's one of the most hated subjects in the world — and it doesn't have to be. The logic is already in you. My job is to help you find it.

I've spent over 8 years teaching high school math at PSUSD, and before coming back to the classroom I spent 4 years as an Instructional Technology specialist working district-wide. That means I've seen education from a lot of different angles, and I bring all of that into how I design my classes.

My classroom has a vibe. You'll notice it. But don't confuse chill with unfocused — we show up, we work, and we figure things out together.

Teaching Philosophy

The win I'm chasing in any classroom isn't a test score. It's the moment when a student who's been telling themselves they "just can't do math" starts asking better questions. That shift in thinking is what the subject is actually for. Math isn't just numbers — it's a way of approaching problems logically, and that skill transfers to everything.

How I Use Technology

I don't use tech as a gimmick. Tools like Desmos, Google Classroom, and AI assistants are only useful when they actually help students understand something better or work more independently. I've spent years helping other teachers figure out when to use technology and when to put it away — and I apply that same judgment in my own classroom.
